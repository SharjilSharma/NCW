Pre-requisites for Pregnant Women

Requirements and consent procedures for pregnant women

Conditions for Conducting Pre-natal Diagnostic Techniques

If a pregnant woman meets any of the criteria mentioned in the qualifying conditions and requires a pre-natal diagnostic procedure, such techniques may only be conducted after all known side effects and potential consequences of the procedure have been clearly explained to her.

Informed Consent Requirements

Following the explanation of risks and procedures, the pregnant woman's written consent must be obtained in a language she fully understands. This ensures that:

  • Full Understanding: The woman comprehends all aspects of the procedure
  • Voluntary Participation: The decision is made without coercion
  • Legal Compliance: All legal requirements are met before proceeding
  • Documentation: Proper records are maintained for legal and medical purposes

Information Disclosure

Before obtaining consent, medical practitioners must provide comprehensive information about:

  • The nature and purpose of the diagnostic procedure
  • Potential risks and side effects
  • Alternative options available
  • Expected outcomes and limitations
  • Post-procedure care requirements

Language and Communication

The Act emphasizes that consent must be obtained in a language the pregnant woman fully understands, ensuring effective communication and genuine informed consent.

Legal Protection

These requirements protect both the pregnant woman and the medical practitioner by ensuring that all procedures are conducted ethically and in compliance with the law.
