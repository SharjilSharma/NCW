Cyber Stalking

Understanding online stalking and harassment laws

What is Cyber Stalking?

Section 354D, IPC states about stalking that includes online stalking. The act of stalking is defined as when a man pursues or approaches a woman despite the woman's clear disinterest in the interaction, or when a man monitors a woman's online behavior by use of the Internet or electronic communication.

Legal Framework

Section 354D of Indian Penal Code

Cyber stalking is specifically covered under this section which addresses:

  • Pursuing or approaching a woman online despite clear disinterest
  • Monitoring a woman's online behavior through internet or electronic communication
  • Persistent unwanted contact through digital platforms
  • Following someone's digital footprint without consent

Punishment

First Conviction: Up to 3 years imprisonment and fine

Subsequent Conviction: Up to 5 years imprisonment and fine

How to Report Cyber Stalking

  • File a complaint at the nearest police station
  • Report online through the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal
  • Contact women helplines for support and guidance
  • Seek legal assistance for proper documentation
