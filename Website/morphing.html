Morphing

Understanding digital manipulation of images without consent

What is Image/Video Morphing?

Photo/video morphing is a method of changing an image or shape by the seamless transition.

Common Morphing Crimes

  • Superimposing a woman's face onto explicit or inappropriate content
  • Altering images to create false scenarios
  • Creating fake intimate or compromising images
  • Manipulating videos to show false activities
  • Using deepfake technology to create realistic but fake content

Types of Morphing

  • Face Swapping: Replacing faces in existing images or videos
  • Body Morphing: Altering body parts or clothing in images
  • Deepfakes: AI-generated realistic but fake videos
  • Context Manipulation: Placing someone in false situations
  • Revenge Morphing: Creating fake content to harm reputation

Legal Framework

Morphing crimes can be prosecuted under:

  • Information Technology Act, 2000
  • Section 66E - Privacy violation
  • Section 67 - Publishing obscene material
  • Indian Penal Code sections for defamation and harassment
  • Sections dealing with outraging modesty of women

How to Report Morphing Crimes

You can make a complaint online with the Ministry of Home Affairs. Online reporting is easier and requires less time than visiting a police station.

Reporting Steps

  1. Document all evidence of the morphed content
  2. Take screenshots and preserve original images
  3. Visit: cybercrime.gov.in
  4. Fill out the complaint form with detailed information
  5. Upload evidence and submit the complaint
  6. Also file a complaint at the local police station

Prevention Tips

  • Be cautious about sharing personal photos online
  • Use strong privacy settings on social media
  • Regularly monitor your digital presence
  • Watermark important personal images
  • Be aware of who has access to your photos
  • Report suspicious activities immediately
  • Educate yourself about digital manipulation techniques
